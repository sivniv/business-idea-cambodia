Executive Summary

The concept is validated — all 3 personas expressed genuine interest in the virtual pet + social map idea. The cat was described as "cute", "fun", "playful", and "less boring than dots". No one found it creepy.

The two critical issues identified are pricing (students max out at $10 while young professionals accept $15-20) and privacy controls (non-negotiable for all). Battery drain was cited as an immediate uninstall trigger.

Key Findings

1. Location Sharing is Already Normal

All 3 users share live location via Telegram for coordination. Typical duration: 15-30 minutes. Only with close friends (3-5 people).

"I shared my live location on Telegram with two classmates. I kept it on for about 15 minutes, then turned it off so no one could see my route back home."— Srey Pov, 20, RUPP Student
"I dropped a Google Maps pin in our group chat, then shared live location for about 30 minutes so everyone could track each other's movements."— Vannak, 24, Software Developer

3. The Cat Concept is Cute, Not Creepy

Vannak: "Fun idea if it's smooth and doesn't drain battery. Unlocking rare cat skins through QR codes feels motivating."

Malis: "The cat makes it less boring than just dots. It's like my little buddy showing where I am!"

Srey Pov: "Cute and fun, especially if the cat changes based on where I go or I can collect rare designs."

5. Battery = Deal Breaker

"If the app drains battery fast, feels laggy, or asks for too many intrusive permissions without good reason — that's a deal-breaker. I'd uninstall quickly."— Vannak, 24

6. Subscription ($1.99/mo) is Acceptable

All 3 were open to paying $1.99/month for: exclusive rare cat skins, enhanced privacy controls, and priority features. Not for purely cosmetic items.

Recommendations

  1. Add a $9.99 digital starter pack for students (rare skin + treats, no hardware).
  2. Publish blind box odds on the box and in-app (1% Legendary, 4% Epic, 20% Rare, 75% Common).
  3. Implement duplicate exchange in the app for social trading loops.
  4. Prioritize battery efficiency — adaptive GPS ping (5min when static, 30s when moving).
  5. Launch at one university (RUPP) before expanding city-wide for density.
